Revamping My Blog

March 1, 2015

What Is This Post About?

I’ve been blogging since 2004. The length span until 2015, doesn’t mean that I’m a good writer. I just like to write.

Started from a handmade blogging platform, circa 2005, that was hosted at .co.nr then they just deleted all of my files. In 2008, I switched to Wordpress. For some reasons, I hate how Wordpress editor looks like. It’s not for writing, it’s for publishing. So there is an issue there. Then, my hosting company, accidentally deleted my files (again) and I assure you, there was no single issue with payment. They only have this limited capacity of brain that I can’t do anything about it. Getting frustrated, I stopped to write.

In 2014, I’ve made myself a blogging platform, called Writ.Ink, a platform that crafted carefully to meet my needs. It’s done. Starting from there, I always take a look at it and see if there’s any problem.

In 2015, a week ago, I’ve concluded few factors that could be my fundamental thoughts about how a blog should resembled. Let me elaborate it.

Standardized Writing Syntax

There is no one, I tell you, no one in this life that would write using HTML.

It would be pretty hard, even for me to write using HTML tags.

Fortunately, there’s Markdown that could simplify my issue. Been using for 2 years and I have no problem with converting back and forth to other formats.

Solution : Start to use Markdown.

Low Cost

Blogging platform should be low cost but not free. Not free because I still need to pay for the domain, emails, etc. In the other hands, the blogging platform I’ll choose should be low cost.

Solution: S3 or Github Pages are the saviors.

Personalisation

Blog should be personal. Personalized enough to show it off to public, in order to distinct yourself among the others. Unfortunately, blogging platform is relatively hard and tough to make it has a personal touch. Although that I am a geek, I don’t have much time to put extra efforts to make it personalized enough. So, platforms that could generate a static files are the answer because you’ll see how your blog looks like in HTML without any implicit linking like Wordpress. (Sorry Matt, it is just too bloated for me.)

Solution: Use Jekyll, Pelican, Octopress

Own The Content

One major problem that I had through my experience is the security of the contents. I’m not talking about how the contents should be stored, using NSA level of encryption, but I’m talking about owning the content. You, I, we should own the content and not to be stored in anyone database. No matter how cheesy your content is, or how scientific it could be, blog posts that you’ve made is a piece of work that you might highly perceive it than any others.

Conclusion

I am happy to announce that I’m back blogging with Jekyll, Markdown and Github Pages. It’s low cost, I own the content, personalized enough and I could write it using any Markdown editors that I have. Plus, with help from Dropbox, I could easily access and build my blog whenever and wherever I feel I need to.
