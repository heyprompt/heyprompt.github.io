Choosing The Right Blogging Platform

August 7, 2015

After doing a several researches regarding the blogging platform, I found many headlines are confusing. Choosing the blogging platform isn't necessarily to be the best one, as nobody does it better than anybody, but it's a matter to suit your needs. Hence, choosing the right blogging platform is considered as a proper title.

Picking the right blogging platform isn't really an easy thing to do. There are numerous blogging platforms out there, both free and paid, lousy and cool. To short down the candidates, I've removed Wordpress, Tumblr and Blogspot and leave Medium, Ghost, Squarespace and Jekyll to compete.

Medium.

Who doesn't know Medium? All cool kids in town are using it without any hesitation. It's beautiful, simple, and without any hassle to write on Medium. However, there are few reasons why I don't like it. It doesn't have any option to pick on your preferred theme. Obviously, it would be really tickling and hilarious stupid if they do that. Blogging for me is a matter of preference. It would be a privilege if I could decide on my own on how it could be presented.

Ghost.

Ghost is really tempting. Starting with $8/month, it seems a really good deal for me to start blogging. However, if I decided to pay for a blogging platform, a mobile application, especially on Android is a must. Yes, it has a good mobile site, but I'm as opportunist as every other user. I pay, I want a mobile app.

Squarespace.

Unlike Ghost, Squarespace is an old player in blogging platform, started since 10 years ago. It has a good Android app as well and I use another application developed by Squarespace for note. You can download it here to experience what I've had. A major problem with Squarespace is it's turning to E-Commerce platform and I worry that in the future, E-Commerce will dominate this platform and leave blogging behind, same what Wordpress have already done.

Jekyll.

Jekyll is not for everyone. It's quite hard to install Jekyll, especially if you don't have any developer background and especially Ruby Gem installation. However, the best thing for Jekyll is it could be hosted on Github, yes, that famous and magnificent source code hosting based on Git, despite the sex scandal fact that happen back in few months ago.

The best thing of using Jekyll is you write and publish your post like as a hacker. You have to do few command like jekyll build, jekyll serve and then do git add -a, git commit and finally git push -u origin master. Quite a long way to blog, isn't it?

Finally.

No, finally isn't another blogging platform. It's a conclusion. I chose to stick with Jekyll. This way, I could save my expenses (approximate $96/annual) and I don't have to worry about image issue since it would be hosted on Github. Looking at Ghost's rapid improvement, I would seriously take another visit on Ghost if they have an official Android application.
